Tell DWP about a change of address

Use this service to tell the Department for Work and Pensions (DWP) about a change of address for:

  • Attendance Allowance
  • Carer’s Allowance
  • Disability Living Allowance (DLA) for adults
  • Pension Credit
This service is also available in Welsh.

You need to wait until you’ve moved before telling DWP about your new address.

To change your address for any other benefit or State Pension, you must contact the relevant team directly.

Before you start

To update your address you will need to sign into your account.

If you do not have an account

You need to register for an account if you’ve not used this service before.

To register, you need:

  • an email address
  • a mobile phone number
  • your National Insurance number

You’ll also have to prove your identity. You’ll need details of 2 types of identity documents for this, for example your:

  • passport
  • driving licence (you cannot use a Northern Ireland driving licence)
  • Self Assessment, if you have one
  • bank accounts, loans, mortgages or credit agreements

If you’ve already registered

You need the email address, mobile phone and password you used to register.

Who cannot use this service

You cannot use this service if:

  • you’re an appointee or manage somebody else’s benefits
  • you live outside of England, Scotland or Wales

If you’re an appointee or manage someone else’s benefits, contact . If you live in Northern Ireland contact NI Direct. If you live outside of the UK (England, Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland) contact .

If you are claiming other benefits or State Pension you will need to contact them directly to tell them about your new address:

  • Universal Credit - contact the Universal Credit helpline.
  • State Pension - contact the Pension Service.
  • Personal Independence Payment (PIP) - contact the PIP helpline at the Disability Service Centre.
  • all other benefits - contact Jobcentre Plus.
